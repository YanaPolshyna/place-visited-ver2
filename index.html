Pearl of Ukraine

Kyiv is the capital and most populous city of Ukraine. It is in north-central Ukraine along the Dnieper River. As of 1 January 2022, its population was 2,952,301, making Kyiv the seventh-most populous city in Europe. Kyiv is an important industrial, scientific, educational, and cultural center in Eastern Europe. It is home to many high-tech industries, higher education institutions, and historical landmarks. The city has an extensive system of public transport and infrastructure, including the Kyiv Metro.

Independance Square

Since the early independence of Ukraine, this square has been a strategic location for political activists, and in 2017 it hosted Eurovision guests from all over the world. View the Independence Monument and the surrounding exhibition in honor of the Heavenly Hundred and continue your walk along Khreshchatyk.

Golden Gate

The most famous defensive fortification of Kyiv, the Golden Gate, was erected during the expansion of the city during the reign of Yaroslav the Wise. The Golden Gate amaze with its grandeur - this huge structure consists of four tiers with wooden superstructures on each side. Monument to Yaroslav the Wise in the park near the Golden Gate

The house with chimeras

The house with chimeras is a unique Art Nouveau mansion decorated with fantastic sculptures (chimeras) of fabulous animals, inhabitants of the underwater world, hunters' trophies. The building is located in the center of Kyiv (in the Pechersky district) near the Administration of the President of Ukraine.

Motherland

Motherland in Kyiv is a majestic and warlike statue of a woman with a sword and shield in her hands, towering in the very center of the Ukrainian capital on the banks of the Dnieper. The monument has become a wonderful symbol of the courage of the people who won the Second World War and continue to fight for their future.
